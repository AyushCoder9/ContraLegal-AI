% ---------- ABSTRACT ----------
\begin{abstract}
Manual review of commercial contracts is a resource-intensive, error-prone, and expensive process that limits access to accurate legal risk assessment. ContraLegal-AI presents a full-stack intelligent legal-tech platform that automates the extraction, classification, explanation, and visual annotation of risk within PDF contracts. The system's architecture comprises three synergistic components: (1) a fine-tuned \texttt{nlpaueb/legal-bert-base-uncased} transformer for three-class risk classification (Low, Medium, High Risk), achieving 97.01\% accuracy and a macro ROC-AUC of 0.9948---surpassing a TF-IDF + Random Forest baseline by 2.57\%; (2) a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline using FAISS vector storage and LangChain for clause-level explanation, rewriting, and conversational contract querying; and (3) a Spatial NLP module leveraging PyMuPDF's coordinate-mapping API to physically annotate risky clauses within the original PDF document. A comprehensive ablation study demonstrates that Legal-BERT provides a critical +11.98\% improvement in High Risk Recall over classical methods---the most consequential metric in legal risk detection. The system is deployed as an interactive Streamlit application with full CI/CD via GitHub Actions.
\end{abstract}

% ---------- INTRODUCTION ----------
\section{Introduction}
In modern commercial environments, legal teams must process vast volumes of contracts---Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs), Master Services Agreements (MSAs), Employment Contracts, and Merger and Acquisition documents---each containing clauses that may impose significant financial liability, asymmetric obligation, or unfavorable jurisdiction. The manual identification of such high-risk clauses demands deep legal expertise and is prohibitively time-consuming.

Prior work in legal text classification has predominantly relied on binary labeling schemes and bag-of-words statistical methods \cite{contract_review_old}. While effective for shallow risk detection, these approaches fail to capture the semantic nuance that differentiates a ``Medium Risk'' indemnity clause from a ``High Risk'' unlimited liability clause. Furthermore, they provide no mechanism for explanation, rewriting, or interactive querying. ContraLegal-AI addresses this gap by delivering an end-to-end automated paralegal system.

% ---------- RELATED WORK ----------
\section{Related Work}
Early legal document automation relied on rule-based systems and regular expression matching for clause identification \cite{contract_review_old}. The introduction of neural embeddings and BERT-family models transformed the field \cite{attention_is_all_you_need}. \texttt{legal-bert-base-uncased}, pre-trained on 12 GB of diverse English legal text, has demonstrated superior domain adaptation compared to general-purpose BERT variants \cite{legalbert}. For retrieval-augmented systems, \citet{rag_lewis} demonstrated that grounding LLM responses in retrieved documents significantly reduces hallucination. The CUAD dataset \cite{atticus} has become the standard benchmark for contract clause classification. Our approach distinguishes itself by combining a domain-specific transformer with RAG and a novel spatial annotation layer.

% ---------- METHODOLOGY ----------
\section{Methodology}

\subsection{Dataset Description}
The system was trained on 21,144 annotated legal clauses derived from the CUAD and publicly available legal corpora \cite{atticus}. A deterministic three-class labeling heuristic was applied: (1) \textbf{Low Risk} (8,328 samples): Clauses with \texttt{clause\_status = 0} in the source annotation; (2) \textbf{Medium Risk} (11,491 samples): Risky clauses with a keyword-weighted risk intensity score $s < 1.5$; (3) \textbf{High Risk} (1,325 samples): Risky clauses where the aggregated keyword weight $s \geq 1.5$.

\subsection{Preprocessing}
All text undergoes normalization via spaCy \cite{spacy}, including stop-word removal, punctuation stripping, and PII masking via regex. For Legal-BERT, raw text is tokenized using the model's WordPiece tokenizer and truncated to 256 tokens.

\subsection{Feature Engineering}
For the baseline model, clause text is vectorized using TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency) with unigram+bigram features capped at the top 5,000 tokens. For the deep learning engine, semantic features are captured through transformer-based self-attention mechanisms \cite{attention_is_all_you_need}.

\subsection{Models Used}
We evaluated two primary architectures:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Random Forest (RF)}: An ensemble of 200 decision trees trained on TF-IDF features.
    \item \textbf{Legal-BERT}: A BERT transformer fine-tuned for three-class sequence classification using a custom \texttt{WeightedTrainer} to address class imbalance.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Training and Validation}
The dataset is split 70/15/15 (train/val/test) with stratification. Legal-BERT was trained for 4 epochs with a learning rate of $2\times10^{-5}$ and early stopping based on validation Weighted-F1. The RAG component uses FAISS for vector storage and LangChain for conversational retrieval.

% ---------- DISCUSSION ----------
\section{Discussion}
The results validate that domain-specific transformer pre-training is critical for capturing semantic risk nuance. The +11.98\% gain in High Risk Recall is the most consequential result, as it directly translates to higher safety in legal review. The three-class system allows for granular prioritization, while the spatial highlighting module bridges the gap between digital analysis and practical utility by providing visually annotated PDFs.

% ---------- CONCLUSION ----------
\section{Conclusion}
ContraLegal-AI demonstrates that intelligent contract risk analysis is achievable through a carefully composed system of transformer classification and RAG. The system fulfills all objectives: Upload, Classify, Explain, and Highlight, providing a production-grade interface for legal professionals.
